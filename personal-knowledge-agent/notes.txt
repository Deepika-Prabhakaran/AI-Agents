AI agents are autonomous systems that can plan, reason, and act toward goals without human intervention.

Large Language Models like GPT and Claude are trained on massive text datasets using transformer architecture.

Vector databases store high-dimensional embeddings and support fast similarity search using cosine or dot-product distance.

RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. It combines a retrieval step with a generative model to produce grounded, accurate responses.

Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting inputs to guide language model behavior toward desired outputs.

Fine-tuning involves training a pretrained model on a smaller domain-specific dataset to improve performance on specific tasks.

Hallucination in LLMs refers to the model generating factually incorrect or fabricated information with high confidence.

The transformer architecture uses self-attention mechanisms to weigh the importance of different tokens in a sequence.
